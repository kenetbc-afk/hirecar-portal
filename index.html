Key Findings & Strategic Notes

CFPB Complaint Channel Strategy

Web submissions to Equifax yield the highest relief rates. Experian web submissions are critically low — certified mail to Fraud & Identity Theft unit is preferred routing.

Metro 2 Field Priorities

Balance / Past Due / Charge-off fields are the strongest dispute targets (objective, verifiable errors). Date of First Delinquency re-aging is per se inaccuracy. Compliance Condition Code (XB/XC/XH) is newest litigation area.

California Dual Jurisdiction

For CA consumers, CCRAA §1785.25 parallels federal FCRA §611 + §623 but adds state-level damages exposure. Always include Layer 5 for California disputes.

FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Dispute Strategy?▼
The dispute strategy is a comprehensive credit repair framework that addresses inaccurate reporting from multiple angles. It combines FCRA Section 611 direct disputes, FDCPA validation demands, state consumer protection statutes, CFPB complaint escalation, and CA State Law overlays (CCRAA §1785) to maximize pressure on furnishers and bureaus to correct or remove derogatory items.
How long does the credit dispute process take?▼
Under the FCRA, credit bureaus have 30 days (or 45 days in certain circumstances) to investigate and respond to a dispute. A full multi-layer strategy typically spans 60–120 days as different layers are deployed strategically over time. Complex cases involving legal filings may take longer depending on court schedules.
What documents do I need to provide?▼
At minimum, upload your credit reports from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion), a government-issued ID, and proof of address. For stronger cases, include any correspondence with creditors, payment receipts, and relevant legal documents such as police reports or FTC Identity Theft Reports.
What is EQFX SWEEP automated extraction?▼
EQFX SWEEP is an automated system that parses your uploaded credit reports (HTML or PDF format) and extracts derogatory items, account details, balances, and reporting dates. This data pre-populates your Derogatory Account Inventory and maps each item to the most effective dispute layers.
What happens after I submit my credit evidence assessment?▼
The CreditWithKen team reviews your intake data and generates a personalized dispute strategy. You receive layer-by-layer recommendations for each derogatory item, including applicable legal theories, recommended dispute channels, and estimated timelines. Court filings and notarized documents are prepared by the HireCar legal team as needed.
Can I track the status of my disputes?▼
Yes. The Documents section tracks all filings, court notices, and correspondence. Each document is assigned a unique control number (HC-YYYY-CAT-NNN) and shows its source. The analytics dashboard provides real-time metrics on your dispute progress.
What is the difference between HireCar and CreditWithKen?▼
CreditWithKen is the service owner that manages your credit repair engagement, intake process, and client relationship. HireCar is the legal operations platform that handles court filings, document control, notarized dispute preparation, and case management. Both work together to deliver comprehensive credit restoration.
Is my information secure?▼
All data transmitted through this portal is encrypted. Documents are stored securely with access restricted to authorized team members. Your credit reports and personal information are handled in compliance with applicable privacy regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does credit repair take?+
Most clients see measurable improvements within 45–90 days. Full programs typically run 4–6 months depending on the number of negative items and bureau response times.
What documents do I need to start?+
You’ll need a valid government-issued ID, proof of address (utility bill or bank statement), Social Security card, and your most recent credit reports from all three bureaus.
Can I dispute items on my own?+
Yes, consumers have the legal right to dispute inaccurate items. CreditWithKen helps you craft effective dispute letters using proven strategies and tracks the entire process for you.
Will credit repair affect my score negatively?+
No. Disputing inaccurate information is your right under the FCRA. Removing negative inaccuracies can only help your score. Your advisor will guide you on which items to prioritize.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a tradeline?+
A tradeline is any credit account that appears on your credit report — credit cards, auto loans, mortgages, student loans, and personal loans are all tradelines.
How do authorized user tradelines boost my score?+
When you’re added as an authorized user on an account with a long positive history and low utilization, that account’s history appears on your credit report, potentially boosting your score.
How long do tradelines take to post?+
Tradelines typically post to your credit report within 1–2 statement cycles (15–45 days) after being added as an authorized user.
What utilization rate should I target?+
Keep individual card utilization under 30%, and ideally under 10% for the best score impact. Your overall utilization across all revolving accounts matters too.

Section 4 of 10

Down Payment Strategy

A strong down payment improves your approval odds and reduces your monthly payment. Here’s how to plan:

  • Minimum: 10% of vehicle price (required by most subprime lenders)
  • Recommended: 15–20% for best rates and lowest payments
  • Trade-in value counts toward your down payment
  • Gift funds may be accepted with a gift letter
Pro Tip

Start saving your down payment as soon as you begin the credit repair program. Even $100/month adds up — by the time your scores are ready, your down payment will be too.

Section 5 of 10

Lender Matching

CreditWithKen works with a network of lenders specializing in different credit profiles. Your advisor will match you based on:

  • Your credit score across all three bureaus
  • Income and debt-to-income ratio
  • Employment history and stability
  • Down payment amount
  • Vehicle type and price range
How It Works

We submit your application to 2–3 matched lenders simultaneously. This creates competition and gives you the best available terms. Multiple applications within 14 days count as a single inquiry.

Section 6 of 10

Vehicle Selection Guide

Choosing the right vehicle is just as important as getting approved. Follow these guidelines:

  • Budget Rule: Monthly payment should be ≤ 15% of your gross monthly income
  • Age: Lenders prefer vehicles under 7 years old with under 100k miles
  • Price: Your advisor will provide a max price range based on your approval
  • Dealers: Work with CreditWithKen-vetted dealers for the best experience
Section 7 of 10

Deal Structure & Negotiation

Once you’ve found your vehicle, your advisor helps structure the deal:

  • Negotiate the out-the-door price (includes tax, title, fees)
  • Review all add-ons and decline unnecessary warranties
  • Confirm the APR matches your approval letter
  • Verify the loan term (aim for 48–60 months max)
  • Get everything in writing before signing
Watch Out

Never share your approval terms with the dealer before negotiating the vehicle price. Negotiate the price first, then discuss financing.
